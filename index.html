About

I was born and raised in Greenwich, CT and have been writing my own songs since I was five years old. When I was a little girl, my CD player was like my teddy bear and I always forced my parents to blast my favorite music in the car so I could sing at the top of my lungs. My dad bought me my first guitar when I was seven years old and I have been playing ever since with my guitar collection steadily growing (thanks dad). When I was eight years old and heard Taylor Swift’s song “Teardrops on My Guitar” on the radio I was hooked by the lyrics, the message and felt truly connected. After reading Taylor’s autobiography and learning she wrote her own songs I was so inspired and knew what I did for fun could turn into my career. Today, my music speaks my truth and serves as my emotional diary. I need to write music to express my emotions, help me to better understand the thoughts running through my mind, and open up to my friends and family. I think there is something beautiful in expressing sentiments through words and music that can reach and connect to others so they know they are not alone. In high school I was fortunate to have been mentored by a world famous songwriter who helped me tremendously to develop as an artist and songwriter. I have performed at “The Bitter End” in New York City, and am now living in Nashville, TN as a songwriting major at Belmont University excited to pursue and grow with my music!
