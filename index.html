About

I was born and raised in Greenwich, CT and have been writing my own songs since I was five years old. Ever since I was a little girl, my CD player was like my teddy bear and I always forced my parents to blast my favorite music in the car so I could sing at the top of my lungs. My dad bought me my first guitar when I was seven years old and I have been playing ever since with my guitar collection steadily growing (thanks dad). However, It wasn’t until I was eight years old, when “Teardrops on My Guitar” by Taylor Swift played on the radio, when I truly connected with the song. It was then that I read Taylor’s autobiography and realized that she wrote her own songs just like me, and I realized that what I did for fun could be my career for the rest of my life. To this day, my music speaks my truth and is my emotional diary. I need to write music because I use my guitar and my lyrics to express my emotions, help me better understand the thoughts running through my mind, and open up to my friends and family. I think there is something beautiful in taking a situation in my life and adding melodies in hopes others can relate to me and have them know they are not alone. My bedroom is my oasis, on Friday nights writing lyrics until my fingers cramp in my journal and then picking up my guitar and adding a melody and chord progressions. My bedroom is my safe space and continues to be the place where I write all of my favorite songs. Through sharing my music with others, I connected with a world famous songwriter who has written numerous songs for well-known artists and has tremendously helped me develop as an artist and songwriter. From there, I have performed at “The Bitter End” in New York City, and am now living in Nashville, TN as a songwriter major at Belmont University.
